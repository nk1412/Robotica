Full Throttle Rules

Download:

☁↓
  • Vehicle Requirements:
    • The robots may be either fully autonomous or remotely controlled.
    • If remotely controlled, the robots must be operated by a human operator who is not physically on the course.
    • The vehicles must be electric and have a maximum 20 cm height and 25 cm width size limit.
    • The exact specifications may vary depending on the competition.
  • Track:
    • The track surface and course line may have unevenness.
    • There will be certain obstacles in the race track which will try to slow down the robot.
  • Race Format:
    • The race may have several rounds, each consisting of a certain number of laps.
    • The fastest vehicle to complete the set number of laps in the shortest time is declared the winner.
  • Safety:
    • Safety is a top priority during the race, and there are strict safety rules in place to ensure that the vehicles do not cause any harm to spectators or other competitors.
    • The vehicles must pass various safety tests and have safety features installed to participate in the race.
  • Technical Inspection:
    • All participating vehicles must undergo technical inspection before the race to ensure that they comply with the rules and specifications.
    • Any vehicle that fails the technical inspection will not be allowed to participate in the race.

Events consist of two rounds:

  • Round 1:
    • The participants will be given a field to race in which they have to show the agility and control over their robot.
    • The field may consist of bumps and turns.
  • Round 2:
    • In this, the qualified players will be given obstacles to face.
    • The obstacles may contain ramps, bridges, zigzag roads, slippery paths, etc.
    • The first 3 participants who complete this will be qualified for selection of the winner and runners.

Team Information:

  • Number of Teams Estimation: 10 to 15
  • Team Size: 2 - 3 members
